## Grandma's Chicken Soup with Dumplings

**Prep:** 20 min | **Cook:** 1.5 hrs | **Serves:** 6

### Ingredients
- 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs)
- 4 carrots, diced
- 4 celery stalks, diced
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 8 cups water
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh dill and parsley

### Dumplings
- 2 cups flour
- 1 tbsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 3/4 cup milk
- 2 tbsp butter, melted

### Instructions
1. Place chicken in large pot, cover with water. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer
2. Cook 1 hour until chicken falls off bone. Remove chicken, shred meat
3. Add carrots, celery, onion, garlic, bay leaves to broth
4. Simmer 20 minutes until vegetables tender
5. Mix dumpling ingredients until just combined (don't overmix)
6. Drop spoonfuls of dumpling batter into simmering soup
7. Cover and cook 15 minutes without lifting lid
8. Add shredded chicken back, season with salt, pepper, fresh herbs

## Pasta Carbonara (Authentic Roman)

**Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 15 min | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb spaghetti or rigatoni
- 6 oz guanciale (or pancetta), cubed
- 4 egg yolks + 2 whole eggs
- 1 cup Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- Black pepper (lots)

### Instructions
1. Cook pasta in well-salted water until al dente (save 1 cup pasta water)
2. Cook guanciale in cold pan on medium until crispy and fat renders
3. Whisk eggs, yolks, and Pecorino together with plenty of black pepper
4. Drain pasta, add to guanciale pan OFF HEAT
5. Pour egg mixture over pasta, toss vigorously (the heat of pasta cooks the eggs)
6. Add splashes of pasta water until silky and creamy
7. Serve immediately with extra Pecorino and black pepper

**Note:** No cream. Ever. That is not carbonara.

## Mom's Chocolate Chip Cookies

**Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 10 min | **Makes:** ~36 cookies

### Ingredients
- 2 1/4 cups flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup butter, softened
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
- 2 eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla
- 2 cups chocolate chips

### Instructions
1. Mix flour, baking soda, salt in bowl
2. Cream butter and both sugars until fluffy
3. Beat in eggs and vanilla
4. Gradually add flour mixture
5. Fold in chocolate chips
6. Chill dough 30 minutes (secret step - don't skip)
7. Drop rounded tablespoons on baking sheet
8. Bake at 375F for 9-11 minutes until edges golden but centers soft
9. Let cool on pan 5 minutes before moving to rack

**The secret:** Slightly underbake. They firm up as they cool.
